"Studying Computer Science in Germany."






Before I entered THM:


I have always had interest in the field of Technology and Computer Science. But since my family is in the field of Restaurant Management, I studied that instead. As I visited my sister who lives here, I fell inlove with Germany. I get to know the culture, way of life and the people. Then I thought, why not stay and study? That's where it all began. I visited a German Language course in Giessen. I had to learn the language at first and it was not that easy. I have met a lot of great people, great company, to sum it all up, I had a real great time.











My journey inside the walls of THM:


My first days in THM wasn't that easy. I have had difficulties understanding the language. The Professors speak so fast that, as Germans say, "Ich verstehe nur Bahnhof." I have to really concentrate and listen. Because of the language, I could say, learning Computer Science is harder for me than most of my classmates. I read books and articles about the subjects both in English and German. I need to manage my time efficiently. Life inside THM is a busy one. Making friends helped me a lot especially in sharing learning materials. So far, even if it's hard, I learn something everyday and it's worth every effort I make. As germans say "Aller Anfang ist schwer."













My plans for the future:


I enjoy coding a lot. I always imagine myself developing softwares, games or writing codes for websites or Robotics. I dream of working with great people and work for the best companies in the world, who wouldn't want that? But I am still deciding what to major in. I research and inform myself about the best companies to work for in my field. Computer Science is an ever evolving field. And even after finishing Computer Science in THM, God Willing, I still plan to continually learn and improve my skills in coding.
